Plitvice Lakes National Park

Published 13.01.2022 by Abdul Muqadam

Plitvice Lakes National Park is a mountainous forest reserve and a UNESCOWorld Heritage Site in central Croatia. It covers a total of 113.9 square miles. The parks features 16 turquoise lakes and several spectacular waterfalls, all of which can freeze duringwinter. The park has crystal clear waters and a delicate ecosystem that is home to species such as brown bears, wild cats, eagles, lynxes, owls, and turtles, as well as 75 endemic plant species

Banaue Rice Terraces

Published 10.01.2022 by Abdul Muqadam

The 2,000-year-old Banaue, or Banawe Rice Terraces, have been called the eighth wonder of the world. The terraces were carved into the Ifugao mountains complete with an irrigation system that taps water from the adjacent forest. Though there is little rice farming on the terraces, tourism is booming as thousands of people come to see the site, as well as interact with the unique Ifugao culture.
